What are the best algorithms to determine  how much and how fast time-series data is changing?
To determine how much and how fast time-series data is changing, you might consider the following algorithms and statistical methods:

1. **Simple Moving Average (SMA)**: Helps to smooth out data and identify the trend by calculating the average of data points over a specified time period.

2. **Exponential Moving Average (EMA)**: Similar to SMA but gives more weight to recent data points, making it more responsive to new information.

3. **Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average (ARIMA)**: A sophisticated model that explains a time series based on its own past values (autoregression), a moving average of past errors, and differencing of raw observations to make the time series stationary.

4. **Seasonal Decomposition of Time Series (STL)**: Breaks down a time series into seasonal, trend, and residual components, which can help understand changing patterns.

5. **Change Point Detection**: Algorithms like CUSUM (Cumulative Sum) or Bayesian methods that can identify points where the statistical properties of a sequence of observations change.

6. **Time Series Clustering**: Groups similar time series together, which can be used to identify changes over time by analyzing the clusters.

7. **Derivative or Differential**: Calculating the first or second derivative of the time series data to determine the rate of change and acceleration, respectively.

8. **Holt-Winters Seasonal Method**: An extension to EMA that can handle both trends and seasonality in time series data.

9. **Dynamic Time Warping (DTW)**: An algorithm useful for measuring similarity between two temporal sequences which may vary in speed.

The choice of algorithm depends on the specific characteristics of the data, such as seasonality, trend, and noise level. For instance, if the data has a clear seasonal pattern, methods like ARIMA with seasonal components or Holt-Winters might be appropriate. If you're interested in sudden changes, then change point detection methods would be most relevant. For smooth trends, SMA or EMA can be suitable. If you need to compare the rate of change between different time series, DTW can be a good choice.
